What this site is

CoinSight is a personal project built to learn how real crypto price data, simple prediction models, and full web apps fit together. It tracks live prices for 14 cryptocurrencies, generates a short-term price estimate for each one, and — maybe most importantly — keeps an honest, public log of how those estimates actually turn out, so you can judge for yourself whether they're any good.

It is not a trading tool, a signal service, or financial advice. It's closer to a transparent experiment in what simple, freely available methods can (and mostly can't) do when it comes to predicting something as chaotic as crypto prices.

How the prediction is actually made

Each prediction blends three simple, well-established statistical and technical-analysis techniques:

  • Trend line — fits a straight line through recent daily prices, weighted so the most recent days count more than older ones, then extends that line one day forward.
  • Momentum — looks at the average daily percent change over the last several days and assumes that pace continues for one more day.
  • Moving-average crossover — a classic technique real traders use: compares a short-term average price to a longer-term one. When the short-term average is above the long-term one, that's traditionally read as a mildly bullish signal, and vice versa.

Rather than picking one method and sticking with it forever, the site tracks how accurate each method has actually been for each specific coin, using its own logged prediction history, and automatically leans more on whichever one has performed better recently. That blend ratio is shown openly on the predictor page — it's not fixed, and it will keep shifting as more real data comes in.

The extra signals you'll see

These are shown for context and transparency — they are not currently used to change the predicted number itself:

  • Fear & Greed Index — a widely-used public sentiment gauge (0–100) reflecting the crypto market's overall emotional mood, built from things like volatility, trading volume, and social activity.
  • Google search interest — how much people are currently searching for a coin, as a rough gauge of public attention.
  • News sentiment — a simple keyword-based scan of recent headlines, counting positive-sounding vs. negative-sounding words. It's intentionally basic and has no real understanding of context or nuance.

Why there's a history page

Every day, the site logs its own prediction for each coin. Once the next day actually happens, that prediction gets compared against the real price. The history page shows this openly — including when the model was wrong, not just when it was right. The goal isn't to make the predictions look impressive; it's to be honest about how hard this problem actually is.

Please read this part

Short-term cryptocurrency price movement is influenced by news, regulation, large trades, and shifts in sentiment that no simple model — including this one — can see coming. Nothing on this site is financial advice, and nothing here should be used as the basis for a real financial decision.
